Mind Science
Neuroscience of raga — brainwave entrainment, psychoacoustics & therapeutic applications
🧠
Brainwave Entrainment
Carnatic ragas modulate neural oscillations through rhythmic structure and melodic contour. Morning ragas like Bhairavi activate theta-alpha transitions (4–10 Hz), while evening ragas like Kalyani sustain alpha-beta coherence.
🫀
Psychoacoustic Response
The Gandhar (Ga) swara's frequency ratios stimulate the limbic system differently. Komal Ga induces parasympathetic activation; Shuddha Ga enhances sympathetic tone and alertness.
🌊
Raga & Circadian Rhythm
Ancient rishi assignment of ragas to specific times reflects chronobiology. Prahar 1 (6–9am) ragas use pentatonic structures matching cortisol surge.
🔬
Gamaka & Motor Cortex
The meend, kan, and sphurita gamakas activate mirror neuron networks. Listeners involuntarily micro-contract laryngeal muscles during gamaka passages.
💊
Nāda Cikitsa
Clinical studies show Raga Darbari Kanada reduces cortisol by 23% in 20-minute sessions. Raga Yaman shows anxiolytic effects in controlled trials.
🎯
Shruti & Microtonality
The 22-shruti system maps onto critical bandwidth theory in psychoacoustics. Intervals smaller than 50 cents trigger dissonance perception.
